Connor Laugs, ENGL 1102, Dr. Lainie Pomerleau, College of Coastal Georgia

2015: The Blue Whale Challenge

⠀Beginning with a real event, Philip Budekin created shock content groups on the Russian social media Vkontakte with videos of death, and stuff in that nature. He'd use these groups to find targets which he felt deserved to be pushed to suicide. In his words, "there are people and there is garbage." He's a man from Russia who began by spreading shock content with people dying, and among those groups he would find people he deemed to be deserving. The first event of this was in November 2015, where Rina would take her life.

Philip would admit to being the person who lead 17 teens to suicide in an interview with St Petersburg news. During this interview, he would also admit to creating the death group chats, named "F57" where he would find victims and talk with them, giving them increasingly more harmful tasks to perform on themselves. With Rina, he didn't state why he felt like she was deserving of it other than "I know (what she did), I just won't tell you this straight. I'll tell you she was a sectarian."

The Blue Whale Challenges are some of the only verifiable incidents where a "curator" for self harm and suicide games has been confirmed. The suicide of Rena Palenkova was one of only 17 of the victims that Philip claimed. He states that the news are attributing many more deaths to him than he had actually even interacted with. The news would report many other "curators" for 50 day challenges like the Blue Whale challenge, but in reality many of them were tweens and teens who played into the memes. This is only the beginning of how yellow journalism gave online cryptids more power than they had.

2017-2020: Momo and Jonathon Galindo

Not much is known about Jonathon Galindo or Momo, because they were essentially carbon copies of the Blue Whale Challenge. Rumours online said that people would change their profile pictures to Momo, and seek out people to "target" for the 50 day game. These rumours were only speculation, and very few if any suicides can be linked to Momo or similarly Jonathon Galindo.

⠀After the news coverage of the Blue Whale challenge died down, similar cryptids would take place Momo and Jonathon Galindo are the best examples of how media sensationalisation has affected parents. While the Blue Whale Challenge itself had verifiable victims and a name associated with the attacks, Momo and Galindo were mostly memed by young people. Parents and police expressed genuine concern, and news stories would exaggerate the effects of the two cryptids. National alerts were being put out warning about the dangers of talking to random people online, and to be wary of these cryptids. These awareness posts would only spread the idea that digital cryptids were online everywhere, and always wanting to target your children. When stories like these are popularised, it diminishes the actual truth behind suicide amongst the youth.

⠀

⠀Even less is known about Jonathon Galindo, because it wasn't as popular of a story for long enough to take form. Jonathon Galindo has very few news reports, but was also treated as seriously as Momo and the Blue Whale Challenge in certain areas. When an 11 year old boy committed suicide in Naples, the news was quick to blame Jonathon Galindo, with the boys last words being "I have to follow the man in the hood." In my research, this is the only connection I can find with Jonathon Galindo and a suicide being “confirmed,” which is a circumstantial piece of evidence at best, as Galindo doesn’t wear a hood. Other than this singular news event, I haven't found any credible news claims that Galindo had anything to do with suicides.

2025-26 Korean Love Game

⠀The Korean Love Game is an example of how heavily addicting social media can be for children. 3 sisters in India ages 12, 14, and 16 became chronically addicted to a "dating app" which preyed on Indian childrens' cultural obsession with Korea. With this "app," it would help them find a "Korean boyfriend." These 3 sisters became so helplessly addicted, they found their only values in being Korean. The girls had, for 2-3 years, become so addicted to their phone and this dating "game" they would stay in their rooms, and even bathe together. While the suicides are real events that are tragic, I feel as if the blame lies mostly on the app itself.

⠀The father describes the things the sisters would do before their passing, a lot of it being extreme isolation for their app. Social media apps have been speculated to be behind depression and anxiety in the youth. Because social media apps are designed to be very addictive, parents should make sure their children have healthy screen habits. A recent court case suing Meta and Google for 3 million dollars proves the precident that it's not unrealistic to conclude that social media has damaging impacts on children's mental states. It can deceptively be easy to see what could've been done to prevent something like this, but it's not the parents' fault for shifting the blame when social media apps are designed to be addictive, and the news exaggerates events that happened, or makes stretches to support a better headline.

In conclusion:

⠀Over time, digital cryptids have become something more than just funny stories on the internet because yellow journalism distracts people from the issue with suicide and an excessive use of social media. Suicide is a very troubling issue among our youth. During the increase in popularity of social media from 2000-2018, suicide rates have increased by 37%. The Blue Whale Challenge happening during the increasing popularity of social media may by correlated, but the digital cryptids aren't the causation. While Philip Budekin did target at least 15 teens, calling them "human waste," social media in itself could be part of the issue with mental health in teens. Facebook/Meta and Google recently lost a lawsuit which concluded the anxiety and depression of a woman was caused by social media's perversive algorithms. The algorithms on social media platforms are built to keep people addicted to them, and it causes depression and anxiety. Rather than shifting blame to cryptids, I hope parents recognise that using social media an excessive amount is more dangerous than Blue Whale-like cryptids.
